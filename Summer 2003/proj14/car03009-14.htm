"Computer Science as a Profession"

Computer science is a discipline that involves the understanding and design of computers and computational processes .

  • General concern is with the understanding of information transfer and transformation.
  • Particular interest is on making processes efficient and with some intelligence.
  • Theoretical studies range from algorithms to practical problems.
  • A central focus is on handling and manipulating information.
Focus

These core ideas provide a discipline designed to develop efficient reliable software and hardware to meet given specifications. Computer science is a young discipline that is evolving rapidly, and includes theoretical studies, experimental methods, and engineering design all in one discipline. In computer science there is an inherent intermingling of the theoretical concepts of computability and algorithmic efficiency which continue to stimulate advances in the discipline.

Key areas of computer science are:
  • theory
  • algorithms
  • data structures
  • programming methodology
  • languages
  • computer elements
  • architecture
  • artificial intelligence
  • computer networking and communication
  • database systems
  • parallel computation
  • distributed computation
  • computer-human interaction
  • operating systems
A computer scientist must have a firm foundation of these princables and be able to apply the fundamental concepts and techniques of computation , algorithms , and computer design to solve specific problems.

The work includeds
  • detailing of specifications
  • analysis of the problem
  • provide designs that function as desired
  • satisfactory performance
  • reliablility and maintainableity of product
  • meet desired cost criteria

Computer scientists must not only have sufficient training in the computer science areas to be able to accomplish such tasks, but must also have a firm understanding in areas of mathematics and science, as well as a broad education in liberal studies to provide a basis for understanding the societal implications of the work being performed.
